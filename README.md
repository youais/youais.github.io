# Yiwei Chai

Hello! I’m Yiwei Chai, a PhD student at Johns Hopkins University. I am interested in contextualising the nature of exoplanets within their systems --- and the broader Galactic population --- through comparative observational studies.

My current projects involve using JWST observations to characterise sub-Neptune atmospheres, and developing end-to-end simulations to predict yields for transiting exoplanet atmosphere science with the upcoming Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope. I also occasionally dabble in the spectroscopy of young directly-imaged giant planets.

Previously, I have investigated the dynamical interactions and spectral composition of a debris disk—brown dwarf system using JWST's MIRI MRS ([paper here](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/ad74f4)), and tested ways to quantify model comparison for exoplanet atmospheres through Bayesian evidence calibration.
